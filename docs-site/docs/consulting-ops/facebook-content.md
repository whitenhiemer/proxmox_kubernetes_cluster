# Facebook Content Runbook

Human-executable. No AI required. Day-to-day posting and engagement for the
ShopStack by Woodhead Tech Facebook Business Page.

**Page setup reference:** `~/WOODHEAD_CONSULTING/marketing/facebook-page-runbook.md`
**Page:** facebook.com/shopstacktech
**Cadence:** 1–2 posts per week
**Best posting times:** Tuesday and Thursday, 10am–12pm or 6–8pm local

---

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Plan the week's post(s) — pick type from rotation below |
| Tuesday | Post #1 of the week (10am–12pm) |
| Wednesday | Engage with any comments/DMs from Tuesday post |
| Thursday | Post #2 if doing 2x/week (optional until you have 5+ posts) |
| Friday | Check metrics, respond to any remaining comments |

Start with 1 post per week until you have a backlog of content that works.
Consistency matters more than frequency at this stage.

---

## Step 1 — Pick a Post Type

Rotate through these types. Don't post the same type back to back.

**Rotation order (repeat):**
1. Pain point
2. Case study / social proof
3. Explainer
4. Behind the scenes
5. Offer / CTA (max once per 5 posts)

Track what you last posted in a note or use the log at the bottom of this file.

## Step 2 — Write or Pull the Post

### Option A: Pull a ready-made LinkedIn post (fastest)

These LinkedIn posts are already written for Facebook — just edit per the tone guide:

| LinkedIn file | Facebook edit needed |
|---------------|---------------------|
| `04-tshirt-case-study.md` | Cut "Ansible playbook" and "Terraform" — say "automated setup" instead |
| `03-shopstack-announcement.md` | Already non-technical, minimal edits |
| `10-shopstack-origin-story.md` | Cut IaC references, keep the story and the $49/mo hook |

**Tone edits every time:**
- Remove: Ansible, Terraform, IaC, Mailcow, Nextcloud, Docker — use plain English equivalents
- Replace closing question with a tag ask or emoji CTA (see templates below)
- Shorten if over 200 words — Facebook readers scroll faster than LinkedIn readers

## Step 3 — Post on Facebook

1. Go to the ShopStack page → **Create Post**
2. Paste the post text
3. If you have a photo (behind-the-scenes type): attach it
4. Do not add external links in the post body — Facebook suppresses reach on posts with links
   - If you need to share a link (e.g., the T-shirt case study), put it in the first comment after posting
5. Click **Post Now** (or schedule for the target time window)

**After posting:** reply to every comment within 2 hours. See Step 4.

---

## Step 4 — Respond to Comments and Messages

**Comments on posts:**

Reply to every comment, even short ones. Early comments boost reach.

| Comment type | How to respond |
|-------------|----------------|
| "Interested" / "INFO" | "Sending you a message now!" → then DM them |
| Question about pricing | Answer directly: "Setup is $800, then $200/month flat. No per-user fees." |
| Tag of a friend | "Thanks [tagger]! [Tagged person], happy to answer any questions — just message us." |
| Skeptical / "what's the catch?" | "Fair question. [Direct answer.] Most people ask about [common concern] — [address it]." |
| Negative / "I'll just use Google" | "Totally fair — Google Workspace works great for a lot of businesses. ShopStack is for owners who'd rather not pay per user or deal with SaaS pricing changes." Don't argue. |

**Direct messages:**

Respond within 1 hour during business hours. Use the [Inbound Lead Response](./inbound-lead-response) runbook.
Facebook DMs are treated the same as any other inbound channel.

Quick qualification: "What kind of business do you run?" before going further.

## Step 5 — Groups Engagement

**Before posting ShopStack content in a group:**
1. Join the group
2. Engage for 7–14 days first — comment on 3–5 posts per week, answer questions
3. Only then post ShopStack content

**When posting in a group:**
- Use Pain Point or Case Study post type
- Add a sentence at the top that's specific to the group:
  > "I work with a lot of DFW small business owners and this comes up constantly..."
- Don't drop a link in the post body (Facebook groups suppress these) — put it in comments if asked
- Respond to every comment within the day

**Target groups (join and engage before posting):**
- DFW Small Business Owners
- Dallas/Fort Worth Entrepreneurs
- Local chamber Facebook group for your city
- Industry-specific groups relevant to your first clients (vet clinics, salons, etc.)

## When You've Run Out of Ideas

If you can't think of what to post:
1. Look at the `linkedin/posts/` directory — anything not yet adapted for Facebook?
2. Think about the last conversation you had with a small business owner — what did they complain about?
3. Post a behind-the-scenes photo — the Beelink box, your home office setup, a deployment screenshot
4. Repost the pinned offer (update the slot count) — works once every 4–6 weeks
5. Ask a question: "What's the most annoying part of running your business tech?" — generates comments and research
