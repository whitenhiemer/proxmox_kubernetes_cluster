# Inbound Lead Response Runbook

Human-executable. No AI required. Covers every inbound channel from first touch
to booked discovery call or qualified disqualification.

**Speed to lead matters.** Respond same day, ideally within the hour. Leads that
don't hear back within 24 hours convert at a fraction of the rate.

---

## Step 1 — Identify the Lead Type

Two products, two very different buyers. Know which you're dealing with immediately.

| Signal | Lead type |
|--------|-----------|
| "email", "invoicing", "files", "small business", "shop", "clinic", "salon" | ShopStack |
| "engineering", "platform", "AI", "automation", "ops", "on-call", "CI/CD" | Engineering consulting |
| "online store", "Etsy", "Shopify", "digital products", "WooCommerce" | ShopStack Online |
| Came from Facebook Business Page | ShopStack (SMB) |
| Came from LinkedIn | Engineering consulting (default) |
| Came from Cal.com booking | Check their company/role in the booking form |

If unclear: ask one question — "What do you do?" — before going further.

---

## Step 2 — First Response by Channel

### Cal.com Booking

They've already booked a time. A booking counts as a qualified lead — they did the work.

Send a confirmation reply within 2 hours:

> Hi [name],
>
> Looking forward to our call on [day] at [time]. To make the most of 30 minutes,
> it'd help to know a bit about your current setup beforehand.
>
> One question: what are you using today for [email / your ops stack / your business tools]?
> Even a sentence helps me come in prepared.
>
> See you then.
>
> Brandon

Do not cancel or reschedule unless absolutely necessary. Show up early.

---

### Facebook Message (ShopStack)

Reply within the hour during business hours. Keep it conversational, not salesy.

**If they asked a question:**
> Hey [name]! [Answer their question directly.] What kind of business do you run?

**If they said "I'm interested" or similar:**
> Hey [name] — great to hear from you. ShopStack is built for small business owners
> who are tired of paying for five different tools. What's your current setup like?
> (Email, file storage, billing, etc.)

**Goal of first reply:** get them to answer one question. That answer tells you
whether they're a fit and opens a natural conversation.

If they respond and it sounds like a fit, move to booking:
> This sounds like exactly the kind of situation ShopStack solves. Want to jump on a
> quick call so I can show you what it looks like for your type of business?
> Here's my calendar: cal.com/brandon-woodward-3nlfbd — takes 30 minutes.

---

### LinkedIn DM (Engineering Consulting)

LinkedIn leads are usually warmer — they've seen your content or profile.

**If they reached out first:**
> Hey [name] — thanks for reaching out. [Acknowledge what brought them here if obvious —
> e.g., "Glad the post on AI integrations resonated."] What's your current ops setup
> like? Happy to dig in if there's something I can help with.

**If they replied to a connection request (you initiated):**
> Thanks for connecting. I've been building AI automation tooling for engineering teams —
> recently shipped alertmind, an alert triage tool that runs between Alertmanager and Slack.
> What does your on-call / ops workflow look like these days?

**Goal:** get a real answer about their stack or pain. One question. Don't pitch yet.

If they describe a real problem, pivot to a call:
> That's the exact kind of thing I work on. Would it be useful if I took 30 minutes
> to dig into it with you? No commitment — just a real conversation about the stack.
> cal.com/brandon-woodward-3nlfbd

---

### Email (Either)

Reply within 4 hours during business hours. Match their tone and length.

**Template:**

> Hi [name],
>
> Thanks for reaching out. [One sentence acknowledging what they said.]
>
> [One question about their current situation — tailor to product type.]
>
> Happy to jump on a quick call if it's easier: cal.com/brandon-woodward-3nlfbd
>
> Brandon

Do not send a wall of text. Do not attach the proposal on first contact.
The goal of the first email is a response, not a close.

## Step 3 — Quick Qualification (before booking a call)

Don't book a discovery call with every lead. Filter for fit first.

**ShopStack — qualify if:**
- Real operating business (not a side project or "idea")
- Currently paying for something ShopStack replaces (Gmail, Google Workspace, Dropbox, PayPal, QuickBooks)
- 1–15 employees
- Has a domain or open to getting one

**Engineering consulting — qualify if:**
- Engineering or technical leadership role (VP Eng, CTO, Platform Lead, Staff+)
- Company has 20–200 engineers
- Describes real ops/automation pain (not "we might want AI someday")
- Series A–C funded or profitable, not pre-revenue

**Pass politely if:**
- "I just need a website" or "Can you build an app for me?"
- Budget mismatch on first message ("what's the cheapest option?")
- Pre-revenue, idea-stage, no current tools to replace
- Looking for an employee, not a consultant

**How to pass:**
> Thanks for reaching out — based on what you've described, I don't think I'm the
> right fit for where you are right now. [Optional: brief reason.] Best of luck with it.

Short, no apology, no explanation loop.

---

## Step 4 — Book the Discovery Call

Once qualified, the goal is a booked call. One link, one ask.

> "Sounds like there's something worth talking through. Here's my calendar —
> grab 30 minutes whenever works: cal.com/brandon-woodward-3nlfbd"

If they don't book within 48 hours of you sending the link, follow up once:
> "Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried — happy to find a time that works
> if the calendar link is inconvenient. What day works best for you this week?"

If no response after that follow-up: move them to the 7-day follow-up sequence (Step 5).

---

## Step 5 — Follow-Up Cadence

Most leads go quiet. That's normal. Don't take it personally and don't give up too fast.

### No response after first message

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Send first response |
| Day 7 | One follow-up — short, direct, different angle |
| Day 21 | Final follow-up — close the loop |
| Day 22+ | Move to cold list, revisit in 90 days |

**Day 7 follow-up (ShopStack):**
> Hey [name] — just circling back. If now's not the right time, no worries at all.
> If you're still thinking about getting the business stack sorted out, I'm here.

**Day 7 follow-up (Engineering):**
> Hey [name] — following up on my last message. Still happy to dig into the ops
> situation if it's still on your radar. What does the next 30 days look like for you?

**Day 21 final follow-up:**
> Wanted to close the loop — if the timing isn't right or this isn't a fit, totally
> understood. Feel free to reach back out whenever. Good luck with [what they mentioned].

After day 21: stop. No more follow-up. Add to 90-day revisit list.

### They booked a call but went quiet after

If a lead booked, then went quiet before the call:
- Confirm the appointment 24 hours before via the channel they prefer
- If they no-show: send one rebook message within 2 hours of the missed time
  > "Looks like we missed each other — no worries. Happy to find another time if
  > you're still interested: cal.com/brandon-woodward-3nlfbd"
- If no response to the rebook within 7 days: move to cold list

### They said "not now" or "maybe later"

Ask: "Is there a better time to check back in?" If they give you one, calendar it.
If they don't, add to the 90-day revisit list and move on.

---

## Step 6 — Track Every Lead

Use a simple spreadsheet or Kanboard. A lead that isn't tracked doesn't exist.

**Columns:**

| Name | Company/Business | Channel | Date | Product | Status | Next action | Notes |
|------|-----------------|---------|------|---------|--------|-------------|-------|

**Statuses:**
`New` → `Contacted` → `Responding` → `Qualified` → `Call booked` → `Proposal sent` → `Closed won` / `Closed lost` / `Nurture`

Update the status after every touchpoint.

## Reference: Response Time Targets

| Channel | Target response time |
|---------|---------------------|
| Cal.com booking | Within 2 hours |
| Facebook message | Within 1 hour (business hours) |
| LinkedIn DM | Within 4 hours (business hours) |
| Email | Within 4 hours (business hours) |
| No-show / missed call | Within 2 hours of missed time |

After hours: respond first thing next business morning.
Do not respond to leads after 9pm or before 7am — sets the wrong expectation.
